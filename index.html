MAT520: Stochastic Differential Equation

Southwestern University of Finance and Economics

Spring 2021

Course Description

This course is designed to introduce theories and applications of stochastic differential equations. A stochastic differential equation (SDE) is a differential equation in which one or more of the terms is a stochastic process, therefore its solution is also a stochastic process. SDEs are widely applied in both natural and social sciences including physics, finance, etc. We will cover both stochastic ordinary differential equations and stochastic partial differential equations. Though it is strongly encouraged that students have already learned or taught themselves the knowledge in ODEs, these are not required for this course. Though I shall provide a quick review when necessary, students registering for this course are expected to have a solid understanding of and obtain skills for the following topics Calculus: Differentiation (product rule, quotient rule, chain rule), integration (by parts), asymptotic expansions(Taylor expansions), some basics of the Lebesgue integration theories such as Dominated convergence theorem, Fubini's theorem Probability: probability spaces and measure spaces, probability distribution functions(pdf), expectation, variation Measure theory: Acquaintance with basic measure theorey is meritous Stochastic process (some of the basics): Brownian motion or the Wiener process This is an advanced course for undergraduates with strong motivations and some topics are at the level for graduates.
